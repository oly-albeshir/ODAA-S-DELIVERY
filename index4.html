Our Cultural Food Gallery

chumbo1

Chumbo(chororsa) is an Oromo traditional food made with Xaafii (Teff) flour, Barbaree (a spicy pepper powder), Dhadhaa (the clarified butter) and Baaduu (the local tangy cheese)

marqa

Marqaa made from barley and teff floor.With ghee butter + barbari sauce and buttermilk on the side. So yummy! Barley and teff are superfood and also Ancient Grains.

bunaqala

Buna Qalaa - literally “the slaughter of coffee”. Buna Qalaa is prepared by frying coffee cherries in traditional butter (dhadhaa).

chachabsa

The word Chechebsa is an Afan Oromo word that means shredding. That is the meal, sliced ​​flatbread with add-ons. It can be as varied as pancakes .

Anchote

Anchote is an endemic root crop of Ethiopia and it is a unique root crop in its uses and the parts consumed

Chuko

Chuko, barley preserved with butter.it's from barley powder mixed with a sufficient amount of distilled butter, along with ginger, onion, salt and spices.
